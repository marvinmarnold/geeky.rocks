Unplugged

Encryption & Mesh networks

Technology has revolutionized the way we do business, changed the shape of our social networks, reshaped our communications and altered the way we spend our time. However, as the importance of connectivity has grown, the internet has become more expensive, less secure, and increasingly inequitable. A 2014 Open Technology Institute study of broadband costs found that that “customers in the U.S. still tend to pay more than their peers in Asia and Europe for comparable broadband Internet service”. Additionally, a 2014 Pew study on public perceptions of online privacy reveals that a majority of respondents feel insecure sharing personal information over social media, but also do not believe it is possible to protect their online privacy.

Unplugged provides a suite of easy to install and use technologies that provide faster, disaster resistant, community driven, and cost effective connectivity. The overarching principal on which Unplugged is based is that given the increasing prevalence of smartphones and smart devices, the internet can be extended and decentralized by sending information device-to-device rather than relying on the cumbersome fiber optic/cell-tower/satellite infrastructure of traditional Internet Service Providers (ISPs). Cutting out such third-party companies takes major steps to reducing infringements on privacy and returning control over communication to the community level. Our products protect users' rights to connectivity and privacy while empowering communities to take ownership of technological infrastructure and facilitating locally-sourced creative solutions to the challenges of our connected and complex world.

Movercado

Mobile outreach for Africa

Movercado is a simple, yet powerful technology that has the power to link aid agencies, markets and populations together into one self-organizing eco-system. By using technologies that are simple and used widely (such as SMS) and yet are infinitely customizable on the back-end (algorithms allowing everything from personalized messaging to advanced data-mining), Movercado provides a powerful base for deep, impactful and cost-effective penetration into markets that currently require a high overhead to reach.

Movercado also powers more complex systems such as tiko or pro, virtualizing processes and workflows that many of us take for granted on premium markets. Not only does this allow impoverished populations to exchange goods and services effectively without cash, it also allows for the management of a large umber of micro-entrepreneurs without significant fixed costs to stores, distribution centers, or aid agencies. Because of its architecture – designed to allow various components to co-evolve continuously – Movercado is evolving very fast and directly responding to needs, opportunities and feedback from the markets.

HydroHarvest

Water sanitation solutions

HydroHarvest is a venture dedicated to bringing clean water to developing countries through cost-effective rainwater harvesting systems. Our team has worked extensively with our community partners in Rwanda in the past and on water projects. We are seeking funds in order to install a rainwater harvesting system at the Maranyundo School this summer as the pilot of our venture. This project will immediately impact the school community of 200 girls and administrators. Our analysis shows that the investment in a system with a collection area of 600m2 breaks even in under 3 years through cost savings from eliminated water and firewood purchases. In addition to the economic benefit, the system will provide important community health benefits and serve as a reproducible model for additional projects providing access to clean water in Rwanda.

OLPC Games

Pedal powered electricity in Haiti

Pedal powered electricity offers a viable source of power for energy in many contexts. At the same time, this form of energy is often misused and leads to forms of undesirable labor where youth are forced to operate against their will.

In April 2010, the Geeky NOLA team piloted a solution to this problem in Haiti. By combining a pedal power system with a cheap computer called OLPC, we turned this process into a video game where players earn points by pedaling at the optimal speed for power generation.
